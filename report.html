Introduction

Game Critics Gathered comes from my love of games and despise of how the gaming industry is run. I have always been a gamer and by that logic, I have always been a game critic. I am also a game designer professionally. I had a chance to witness the industry from both sides and one thing that has always bothered me, is how much non-disclosed advertisement there is in the gaming industry. I have always been a fan of the idea of democratizing game criticism. I believe that the best way to do that is to create a platform where anyone can post their reviews and opinions about games. As I said in the F.A.Q, The most important goal of a game critic is to inform the purchase of the audience. I wanted to design a website that would allow anyone to post their reviews and opinions about games and allow the audience to make their own decisions.

By this logic, I have designed a flow for a game critic that will allow them to post their honest reviews and opinions about games. I have also designed a flow for the audience that will allow them to read the reviews and opinions of other game critics and make their own decisions. I wanted to create the website in such a way that no criticism is buried under the other and that the audience can easily find the reviews and opinions that they are looking for.

This is the reason of big emphasis on the search engine, dropdown menus and the search page. As another way to democritize game criticism, I have also designed a submission page that will allow anyone to submit their reviews and opinions about games. I also wanted to have a very simple and clean design for the website. This is another reason of my emphasis on accessibility.

Inspiration

  1. Steam Comments: Steam game marketplace has a comment button under each game
  2. That Video Game Blog is a blog that i follow.
  3. Video Game Dunkey is a game reviewer I really like to follow who is very honest and to the point.

Usability

  1. Search & Posts Page: I have created a search page that will help users navigate asround the website easily.
  2. Clear Design: I have adopted for a cleaner, breathing room filled design that give users more focus on where they wish.
  3. Slideshow & Filters: I wanted to give the control of their content back to the user.

Accessibility

  1. No Overflowing of the text or images at 500% zoom-ins.
  2. Dark-mode and scroll to the top features.
  3. Contrasting colors and images at dark or light mode.

I learned...

  1. How to work with asynchronous functions and loading times.
  2. How to save and get information from session or local storages.
  3. Create responsive website layouts with flexbox.

Evaluation 1 - Successful at ...

  1. creating the search page and search results by providing as much result as possible, giving users freedom
  2. creating a useful website that has circular paths, meaning the user can go from anywhere to anywhere
  3. creating a website that is accessible and responsive with flexbox properties and media queries.
  4. creating a fitting design for my theme that champions game characters.

Evaluation 2 - I would wish to improve...

  1. my design and layouts and give more neat structure
  2. my color choices. I feel this is my Achilles heel.
  3. my earlier JavaScript code.
  4. keybaord navigation of my website.
